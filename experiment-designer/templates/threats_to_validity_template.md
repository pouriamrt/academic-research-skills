# Threats to Validity Template — Comprehensive Validity Assessment Matrix

## Purpose

A fill-in template for systematically assessing all threats to validity for an experimental design. Based on the Campbell and Stanley (1963) and Shadish, Cook, and Campbell (2002) frameworks. Covers four categories: internal, external, construct, and statistical conclusion validity. Produced by the design_architect_agent and included in the experiment protocol.

## Instructions

1. For each threat, assess whether it applies to your specific design
2. Rate the likelihood as High, Medium, or Low based on your design features
3. Describe the specific mitigation strategy you are using
4. Assess the residual risk remaining after mitigation
5. Mark threats as "N/A" only if they are genuinely inapplicable (not just unlikely)
6. Every design should have at least 3-5 threats rated Medium or High — if you have none, you are underestimating risks

## B. External Validity Threats [Required]

External validity = confidence that results generalize beyond the specific study conditions.

| # | Threat | Description | Applies? | Likelihood | Mitigation Strategy | Residual Risk |
|---|--------|-------------|----------|-----------|-------------------|---------------|
| E1 | **Population validity** | Results may not generalize to populations different from the study sample (age, demographics, culture, clinical severity) | [Yes/No/N/A] | [H/M/L] | [e.g., Diverse sampling / Report sample demographics for reader judgment / Replication across populations] | [e.g., Sample is from a single university; generalization to other contexts is limited] |
| E2 | **Ecological validity** | Results may not generalize to settings different from the study context (lab vs real-world, controlled vs naturalistic) | [Yes/No/N/A] | [H/M/L] | [e.g., Conduct in naturalistic setting / Minimize artificial constraints / Use real-world tasks] | [Residual risk] |
| E3 | **Temporal validity** | Results may not generalize to different time periods (historical, seasonal, policy era) | [Yes/No/N/A] | [H/M/L] | [e.g., Replicate at different time points / Acknowledge temporal limitations] | [Residual risk] |
| E4 | **Treatment variation** | The treatment may work differently when delivered by different people, in different formats, or at different doses | [Yes/No/N/A] | [H/M/L] | [e.g., Manualized treatment / Fidelity monitoring / Multi-site replication / Report delivery details] | [Residual risk] |
| E5 | **Outcome measurement generalizability** | Results on the specific outcome measure may not generalize to other measures of the same construct | [Yes/No/N/A] | [H/M/L] | [e.g., Multiple outcome measures / Use validated instruments with known generalizability] | [Residual risk] |
| E6 | **Interaction of selection and treatment** | Treatment may work for volunteers but not for a general population (volunteer bias) | [Yes/No/N/A] | [H/M/L] | [e.g., Compare volunteer characteristics to population / Use mandatory participation where ethical] | [Residual risk] |

## C. Construct Validity Threats [Required]

Construct validity = confidence that the variables measured/manipulated represent the intended theoretical constructs.

| # | Threat | Description | Applies? | Likelihood | Mitigation Strategy | Residual Risk |
|---|--------|-------------|----------|-----------|-------------------|---------------|
| C1 | **Mono-operation bias** | Using only a single operationalization of the independent variable (one manipulation) limits construct representation | [Yes/No/N/A] | [H/M/L] | [e.g., Multiple treatment conditions varying the construct systematically / Component analysis] | [Residual risk] |
| C2 | **Mono-method bias** | Measuring the DV with only one method (e.g., only self-report) confounds construct with method | [Yes/No/N/A] | [H/M/L] | [e.g., Multi-method assessment: self-report + behavioral + physiological / Triangulation] | [Residual risk] |
| C3 | **Hypothesis guessing** | Participants guess the study hypothesis and alter their behavior accordingly (demand characteristics) | [Yes/No/N/A] | [H/M/L] | [e.g., Blinding / Cover story / Post-study hypothesis awareness check / Implicit measures] | [Residual risk] |
| C4 | **Experimenter expectancy** | Researcher's expectations unconsciously influence how they interact with participants or score outcomes (Rosenthal effect) | [Yes/No/N/A] | [H/M/L] | [e.g., Double-blinding / Standardized scripts / Automated outcome measurement / Blinded outcome assessment] | [Residual risk] |
| C5 | **Confounding constructs** | The treatment package contains multiple elements, and it is unclear which element causes the effect | [Yes/No/N/A] | [H/M/L] | [e.g., Dismantling design / Active control that includes non-target elements / Component analysis] | [Residual risk] |
| C6 | **Social desirability** | Participants respond in ways they think are socially acceptable rather than truthfully | [Yes/No/N/A] | [H/M/L] | [e.g., Anonymous administration / Social desirability scale / Implicit measures / Behavioral outcomes] | [Residual risk] |
| C7 | **Reactivity to experimental situation** | Participants behave differently simply because they know they are in a study (Hawthorne effect) | [Yes/No/N/A] | [H/M/L] | [e.g., Active control group (both groups know they are in a study) / Unobtrusive measures / Long study duration for habituation] | [Residual risk] |
| C8 | **Inadequate preoperational explication** | The construct is poorly defined, leading to measurement that does not fully capture it | [Yes/No/N/A] | [H/M/L] | [e.g., Clear construct definition grounded in theory / Expert review / Pilot testing] | [Residual risk] |

## D. Statistical Conclusion Validity Threats [Required]

Statistical conclusion validity = confidence that the statistical analysis accurately detects (or fails to detect) a relationship.

| # | Threat | Description | Applies? | Likelihood | Mitigation Strategy | Residual Risk |
|---|--------|-------------|----------|-----------|-------------------|---------------|
| S1 | **Low statistical power** | Insufficient sample size to detect a true effect, leading to Type II error (false negative) | [Yes/No/N/A] | [H/M/L] | [e.g., A priori power analysis targeting power >= 0.80 / N = [value] per group] | [Residual risk: if effect is smaller than expected, power drops below target] |
| S2 | **Violated assumptions** | Statistical test assumptions (normality, homogeneity of variance, independence) are not met | [Yes/No/N/A] | [H/M/L] | [e.g., Assumption checking protocol / Robust alternatives (Welch's, bootstrap, non-parametric)] | [Residual risk] |
| S3 | **Fishing / p-hacking** | Running multiple tests without correction inflates the probability of false positives | [Yes/No/N/A] | [H/M/L] | [e.g., Pre-registration / Bonferroni/FDR correction / Distinguish confirmatory vs exploratory analyses] | [Residual risk] |
| S4 | **Unreliable measures** | Measurement error (low reliability) attenuates observed effect sizes and inflates noise | [Yes/No/N/A] | [H/M/L] | [e.g., Use instruments with alpha >= 0.70 / Multiple items per construct / Disattenuated effect sizes] | [Residual risk] |
| S5 | **Range restriction** | Restricted variability in one or both variables attenuates the observed relationship | [Yes/No/N/A] | [H/M/L] | [e.g., Sample from full range of the construct / Correction formula for restriction] | [Residual risk] |
| S6 | **Inaccurate effect size estimation** | Effect size from prior research may be inflated (publication bias, winner's curse) or from a different context | [Yes/No/N/A] | [H/M/L] | [e.g., Use meta-analytic estimate / Apply shrinkage / Conservative effect size assumption] | [Residual risk] |
| S7 | **Extraneous variance in the setting** | Noise from the experimental environment (distractions, inconsistent conditions) obscures the true effect | [Yes/No/N/A] | [H/M/L] | [e.g., Standardized procedures / Controlled environment / Random assignment (distributes noise equally)] | [Residual risk] |
| S8 | **Heterogeneity of units** | Large variability among participants makes it harder to detect treatment effects | [Yes/No/N/A] | [H/M/L] | [e.g., Homogeneous sampling / Blocking / ANCOVA with pretest / Within-subjects design] | [Residual risk] |
